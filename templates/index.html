Computer Vision - Extract Texts and Search Images

InnoGuard uses computer vision to grab texts from screenshots taken from e.g. Whatsapp Messaging apps. Futhermore, Google's Vision API is used to determine Racy and Violent Images sent to a child.

Checks for Malicious and Adult Sites

Google's Safe Search and Safe Browsing APIs checks for any dangerous and adult sites a child make click on .

Sentiment Analysis

InnoGuard uses Tisane API, which determines death treats, personal attacks, harassment, sexual abuse, hate speech and online grooming.

Professor Patrick Hosein

CEO

Patrick attended the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) where he obtained five degrees including a PhD in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science. He has worked at Bose Corporation, Bell Laboratories, AT&T Laboratories, Ericsson and Huawei. He has published extensively with over 100 refereed journal and conference publications. He holds 40 granted and 42 pending patents in the areas of telecommunications and wireless technologies. He was nominated for the Ericsson Inventor of the Year award in 2004, was the Huawei US Wireless Research Employee of the year for 2007 and is a 2015 Anthony Sabga Caribbean Laureate for Science and Technology. Patrick is presently the administrative and technical contact for the TT top level domain, CEO of the TTNIC and a Professor of Computer Science at the University of the West Indies. His present areas of research include radio resource management, QoS and pricing for 5G cellular networks.

Gabriela Sewdhan

Lead and Data Scientist

Experienced Data Scientist, Software Engineer and Full-Stack Developer, with degrees in Computer Science and Psychology, possessing a strong technical and artistic background in backend, software and graphic design. Gabriela's passion lies in machine learning, robotics and software engineering, which allows her to accomplish her goal of creatively bringing to life solutions to a wide range of problems. She has experience building and deploying machine learning models for customer satisfaction at Telecommunication Services of Trinidad and Tobago Limited (TSTT) and has developed a mathematical model and a smart, predictive tool that visualises teams’ and organisations’ productivity, based on COVID-19’s company data. She has also worked in teams to develop speech recognition models for Caribbean Dialect, a fully fledged apartment rental and social network app designed for University students, a web app which leverages the Raspberry Pi, Cloud Technologies and React to detect oceanic plastics, and waste using Object Recognition and an automated hydroponics control system that is also packaged as a monitoring tool for plant upkeep, that uses Object Recognition to diagnose plants.

Michael Ali

Data Scientist and DevOps Engineer

Michael Ali’s skills lie in machine learning, particularly computer vision, and in the development and deployment of cloud services. He has worked with insurance companies to release an accurate and scalable OCR and HTR service, dealing with the building of the image recognition model, deployment of the microservices and acting as scrum master in organising and assigning tasks and releases. He is currently employed at TSTT (Telecommunications Services of Trinidad and Tobago) as a Data Science Professional, where he uses his skills to develop predictive models and tools to help executives make informed decisions regarding the company’s structure, finances and future. His experience with SCRUM, Agile methodology and in developing similar end-to-end systems will be highly beneficial to the team.

Rajeev Rattan

Data Scientist and Computer Vision Engineer

Rajeev Ratan is a Data Scientist who obtained his MSc. in Artificial Intelligence from the University of Edinburgh and a BSc. in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of the West Indies. He has worked in several London tech startups as a Data Scientist, Computer Vision specialist, and Software Developer. He was a cohort member of Entrepreneur First, a London based startup incubator where he was the co-founder of an Edtech startup. Later on he worked in tech-startups involved in the Real Estate and e-Gambling sectors. He has created several online courses on Data Science and Computer Vision on Udemy, Packt Publishing, and Manning Publications. Before venturing into Data Science, Rajeev worked as a Radio Frequency Engineer for 8 years at both TSTT and Digicel. His research interests include Probabilistic Modeling, Deep Learning, and Computer Vision. At present, he works as a Data Science consultant and online course creator.

Shiva Ramoudith

Data Scientist and Software Engineer

Shiva is currently a Data Scientist assigned to the CIBC FICB project. He is also a part-time tutor for the Department of Computing and Information Technology at the UWI. Previously, he worked as a software developer on the AgriNeTT and CSRP projects while completing his undergraduate degree. Shiva was also a part winning team for the bmobile Code Challenge in 2019. His interests lie in data science, cybersecurity, and teaching.
